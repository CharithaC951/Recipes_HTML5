Dad's Double Whole Grain Pancakes


Description

A pancake is a flat, round cake typically made from a batter of flour, eggs, and milk, fried on a griddle or in a pan. It's a popular breakfast item, often served with toppings like maple syrup, butter, or fruit. Pancakes can be thick and fluffy or thin and crepe-like, depending on the recipe and region.

Pancakes are enjoyed worldwide with regional variations. For example, in Japan, okonomiyaki is a savory pancake with cabbage and other ingredients, while in India, dosas are a fermented pancake.


Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 ⅓ cups dry milk powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 ½ teaspoons baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 cups whole wheat flour
  • ¾ cup white sugar
  • 4 eggs, lightly beaten
  • 3 cups water
  • ¼ cup butter, melted
  • 3 tablespoons vinegar

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, sift all-purpose flour, milk powder, baking powder, baking soda and salt. Stir in whole wheat flour.
  2. In a small bowl, combine sugar, eggs, water, butter and vinegar. Make a well in the flour mixture, and pour in the egg mixture. Mix until smooth.
  3. Heat a lightly oiled griddle or frying pan over medium heat. Pour or scoop the batter onto the griddle, using approximately 1/4 cup for each pancake.
  4. Cook until pancakes are golden brown on both sides; serve hot.
